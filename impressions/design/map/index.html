Map

You Are Here

Our story takes place in western Massachusetts, in the valley around the Connecticut River, between the Vermont/Massachusetts border to the north and the Connecticut coastline to the south.

The Interactive Map

The interactive map feature lets you geographically explore the history and science of the discovery of dinosaur tracks. Markers indicate relevant places of interest; Edward Hitchcock's geologial maps and ice age information can be displayed.

Show Maps

These check-boxes let you turn map layers on and off, displaying Hichcock's maps as well as Lake Hitchcock and the glacier from the last ice age.

Markers

Clicking a marker on the map opens a window with information about that site. Links to markers are also displayed to the right of the map, in the "Marker Links" field.

How to Use the Map

Show Markers

There are three categories of map markers: "Fossils & Geology" sites, "Places to Visit" and "Historical Sites". Selecting one of these radio buttons will plot the relevant markers on the map.

Marker Links

These links correspond with the markers. Clicking of of these links will center the map on the chosen marker and open the window with more information about that site.

Dig Deeper

These links open windows with information that's related to the Marker category currently being displayed.
